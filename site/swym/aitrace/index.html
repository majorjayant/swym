Where AI output was not good enough

Twice, in the same Phase 1 report — both caught by me, both recorded as errors in the log (Entry 009):

  1. Skipped a required stress test. My Phase 1 spec said any field whose name implies a grouping must be stress-tested, not trusted. The AI's profiler hard-coded a candidate list that missed terminal_session_id and then reported "3.13 events/session avg" as if it were a session. The forced rerun showed groups spanning multiple branches (86/150) with a median span of ~16 days — not a session at all. Two options died on that fact.
  2. Tested a relation its own finding contradicted. It found all 120 terminal-bearing events land strictly before same-terminal logins, then tested for events inside login windows, got zero, and concluded "no join possible." I made it run the pre-login window test its own anomaly pointed at: deterministic 1:1 linkage, zero ambiguity at 45 minutes. The conclusion flipped from "impossible" to "possible but deliberately deferred."

A decision I did not delegate, and why

Choosing what to build. The operating rules I wrote before the clock started forbade the AI from choosing the problem, ranking options, or saying "I recommend." It surfaced 8 options with trade-offs; I triaged all 8 (T+59), ran RICE on my shortlist, and chose 2 + 3 + 8 on a Kano framing (T+61). The AI then argued against my choice — once, per protocol: three partials instead of one finished build, and option 3's thin evidence base. I kept all three, and that disagreement is preserved in SCRATCH.md rather than smoothed over. The problem definition is the assessment; it stayed mine.

Also not delegated: the T+68 scope freeze (a rule I imposed on the AI, which it enforced against me at T+71 — overrule logged), and every decision-log entry, which the AI transcribed but never authored.
