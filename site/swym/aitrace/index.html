Where AI output was not good enough

Both instances kept, both timestamped in the log:

  1. T+33 — the Phase 1 double miss (Entries 008/009). My spec said any field whose name implies a grouping must be stress-tested, not trusted. The AI's profiler hard-coded a candidate list that missed terminal_session_id and reported "3.13 events/session avg" as if it were a session — the forced rerun showed 86/150 groups spanning multiple branches, median span ~16 days. And it tested a relation its own finding contradicted: all 120 events land strictly before same-terminal logins, it tested inside windows, got zero, concluded "no join possible." I made it run the pre-login test its own anomaly pointed at: deterministic 1:1 linkage, zero ambiguity at 45 minutes. The conclusion flipped from "impossible" to "possible but deliberately deferred."
  2. T+25 — it assumes past its limits (Entry 008). If anything goes beyond its limit it assumes and starts going. I set a 100-line scope gate; it wrote a 181-line script anyway, flagging mid-flight: "Script will exceed the 100-line gate — the requirements list (A–G) is broad and I'd rather run one comprehensive pass than three partial ones. Flagging that override; if you want it split, say so and I'll refactor." If agents and all of the setup exists it would become easier for it to call its subagents and do all of it and then cumulate and verify it.

A decision I did not delegate, and why

The option triage and prioritization. My dispositions on all 8 options (T+59) came before any framework existed; RICE and Kano were run at my order with my impact/effort assumptions, and the AI was forbidden throughout from ranking or recommending. It surfaced options with trade-offs; the calls were mine. The AI then argued against my 2+3+8 choice — once, per protocol: three partials instead of one finished build, and option 3's thin evidence base. I kept all three; the disagreement is preserved in SCRATCH.md rather than smoothed over. The problem definition is the assessment; it stayed mine.

Also mine: overruling the T+68 scope freeze the AI enforced against me — it argued once, I ordered the build, the overrule is logged. And every decision-log entry, which the AI transcribed but never authored.
